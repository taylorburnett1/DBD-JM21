The 21 Irrefutable
Laws of Leadership

John C. Maxwell

Foundational principles for becoming a leader of influence — built on character, trust, and the relentless development of yourself and others.

Explore the Laws

The 21 Laws

Follow them and people will follow you.

1
Law 01
The Law of the Lid
Leadership ability = your ceiling
Leadership ability determines a person's level of effectiveness. The lower your ability to lead, the lower the lid on your potential.
2
Law 02
The Law of Influence
Nothing more, nothing less
The true measure of leadership is influence. Leaders must inspire and motivate others — title alone means nothing.
3
Law 03
The Law of Process
Daily, not in a day
Leadership develops daily, not in a day. It is a lifelong process that requires continual improvement and disciplined growth over time.
4
Law 04
The Law of Navigation
Chart the course
Anyone can steer the ship, but it takes a leader to chart the course. Leaders plan ahead and navigate obstacles before they arise.
5
Law 05
The Law of Addition
Serve to lead
Leaders add value by serving others. The best leaders focus on helping those they lead grow, succeed, and reach their full potential.
6
Law 06
The Law of Solid Ground
Trust is the foundation
Trust is the foundation of all leadership. A leader must demonstrate consistent integrity to earn — and keep — the trust of others.
7
Law 07
The Law of Respect
Earned, never given
People naturally follow leaders stronger than themselves. Respect is earned through actions, character, and consistent demonstration of ability.
8
Law 08
The Law of Intuition
Read the room
Leaders evaluate everything with a leadership bias. They develop a keen instinct for reading people, situations, and the dynamics of a room.
9
Law 09
The Law of Magnetism
You attract who you are
Who you are is who you attract. Leaders draw others who share similar values, attitudes, and characteristics — for better or worse.
10
Law 10
The Law of Connection
Heart before hand
Leaders touch a heart before they ask for a hand. Building genuine relationships is the foundation of moving people toward any vision.
11
Law 11
The Law of the Inner Circle
Your circle defines your ceiling
A leader's potential is determined by those closest to them. Surrounding yourself with the right people is one of the most critical decisions you'll make.
12
Law 12
The Law of Empowerment
Secure leaders give power away
Only secure leaders give power to others. Empowering your team creates a more capable organization — and reflects true confidence in your leadership.
13
Law 13
The Law of the Picture
They see before they follow
People do what people see. Leaders must model the behavior, work ethic, and character they expect from those they lead — every single day.
14
Law 14
The Law of Buy-In
Leader first, vision second
People buy into the leader before they buy into the vision. Earn trust and loyalty first — then lead people toward what matters.
15
Law 15
The Law of Victory
Find a way to win
Leaders find a way for the team to win. True leadership isn't about personal glory — it's about delivering results and making others successful.
16
Law 16
The Law of the Big Mo
Momentum is your best friend
Momentum is a leader's best friend. Great leaders create and protect momentum — because once it's lost, it takes far more energy to rebuild it.
17
Law 17
The Law of Priorities
Activity ≠ accomplishment
Leaders understand that activity is not necessarily accomplishment. Identifying and protecting the highest-value tasks separates great leaders from busy ones.
18
Law 18
The Law of Sacrifice
Give up to go up
Growth and success require real sacrifice — comfort, ego, time, and sometimes relationships that no longer serve the mission.
19
Law 19
The Law of Timing
When matters as much as what
When to lead is as important as what to do and where to go. The right action at the wrong time still fails. Great leaders read the moment.
20
Law 20
The Law of Explosive Growth
Lead leaders, not just followers
To add growth, lead followers. To multiply growth, lead leaders. Developing other leaders creates exponential impact beyond what any single leader can produce.
21
Law 21
The Law of Legacy
What you leave behind
A leader's lasting value is measured by succession. The greatest leaders build something that outlasts them — in people, culture, and impact.
"

A leader is one who knows the way, goes the way, and shows the way.

— John C. Maxwell
